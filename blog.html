a Walk in Area 10

The diverse range of Malawi birding right on your doorstep in Lilongwe, Malawi.

Eugene's Birdocs - a Walk in Area 10

White-Browed Robin-Chat with grub.

short distances, tall trees

Visiting a country like Malawi, you will always find yourself at first, within the city. But Lilongwe to me, is more like a town. Yes, it is large and have lots of cars but having no tall buildings filling the horizon makes it feel a tad bit different. But booking a lodging, make sure that it will take you to the residential side of Lilongwe. 

Here you will be welcomed by green gardens, tall trees and most of which are still native trees and plants. That is a massive attraction to birds that are found in this range. You will be amazed by the amount of birds you will find here in Lilongwe. I would say that most residents here still add birds to their Lilongwe garden lists. 

for the love of diversity

Your first introduction will be the amount of beautiful song these birds can conjure. Your head will be going from side to side trying to listen and see who is making all these sounds. I have been to many cities through the years, but Lilongwe offers a more peaceful birding session than most. Malawi is very laid back and you can feel it. This visit was in the peak of the rainy season, this is generally a good time of year for birding. Migrants come down from more north in Africa as well as some birds from Europe. 

Schalow's Turaco, enjoying the greenery.

peace on your soles

It is not always that a walk around town can make you feel like you are on holiday. Avoiding the main roads here can make a big difference in your walk. It relatively slows down when you decide to go off track. Just have your binoculars out and watch the trees overhanging the fences for any movement. I've seen a lot of birds here in my stay, but there is always a new surprise, the other day I saw a Collared-Palm Thrush on the roof just outside the kitchen door!

kaza kitchen on heavenly close

It's good to have a break from your long walk. Kaza Kitchen is found here in Area 10 and is a cafe found at the end of the road named Heavenly Close. Along this road you will be joined by a stream going down to the dam, and a lot of beautiful trees giving the road some shade. I enjoyed walking off the road and scouting the thicket for birds found along the stream. 

Black Crake running along the reeds.

streams to damn street dam

Throughout the year, dry and wet, this dam is a water source for a lot of the birds who nest in the area. Here you can find a variety of birds that depends on the habitat made by the dam and streams. When the rain falls on the dry landscapes early in the rainy season, all streams lead down to the dam found on Blantyre Street, Area 10, Lilongwe. In my own review I would say Lilongwe in Malawi is a paradise for birders. There are so many places to walk to and visit. I hope to cover more of the gems found here.

Red-Chested Cuckoo, warming up for a good song.
